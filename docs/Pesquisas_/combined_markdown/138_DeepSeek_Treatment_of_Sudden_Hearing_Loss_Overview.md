### **Clinical Definition and Urgency**

**Sudden Sensorineural Hearing Loss (SSNHL)** is most commonly defined as a sensorineural hearing loss of **‚â•30 dB** affecting **at least three consecutive frequencies** that occurs **over a 72-hour period or less**. It is a medical emergency.

### **First-Line Treatment: Corticosteroids**

The cornerstone of treatment for idiopathic SSNL (where no specific cause is found, which is about 90% of cases) is corticosteroid therapy, which reduces inflammation and swelling in the inner ear.

#### **1. Systemic Corticosteroids**
*   **Protocol:** Oral Prednisone, typically starting at a dose of **1 mg/kg/day** (often a 60 mg daily dose) for 7-14 days, followed by a taper over a similar period.
*   **Evidence:** This has been the standard of care for decades, largely based on clinical experience and historical practice. A landmark study (the "Weiss" study) in 1980, while methodologically flawed by modern standards, solidified its use.

#### **2. Intratympanic (IT) Corticosteroids**

*   **Advantages:**
    *   Delivers a much higher concentration of the drug to the target site (the cochlea).
    *   Avoids the systemic side effects of oral steroids (e.g., hyperglycemia, insomnia, mood swings).
